Before investing in technology, websites, or campaigns, organizations need clarity. The Logic Audit is a diagnostic framework that maps goals, users, constraints, and success metrics—ensuring alignment before execution.

The Problem: Projects Without Foundations

Most digital transformation projects fail not because of poor execution, but because of poor planning. Organizations rush into building websites, implementing systems, or launching campaigns without first understanding:

  • What problem they're actually solving
  • Who the end users are and what they need
  • What constraints (budget, timeline, capacity) exist
  • How success will be measured

This leads to expensive mistakes: systems that nobody uses, websites that don't convert, campaigns that miss the mark. The Logic Audit framework prevents this by forcing clarity before commitment.

What Is a Logic Audit?

A Logic Audit is a structured diagnostic process that maps four critical dimensions of any digital project:

The Four Pillars of a Logic Audit

  1. Goals & Objectives: What are you trying to achieve? What does success look like?
  2. Users & Stakeholders: Who will use this? What are their needs, behaviors, and pain points?
  3. Constraints & Context: What are your budget, timeline, technical, and organizational limitations?
  4. Success Metrics: How will you measure progress and outcomes?

By answering these questions upfront, organizations gain the clarity needed to make informed decisions about scope, approach, and investment.

Why It Works

The Logic Audit framework is effective because it:

  • Prevents scope creep: Clear goals and constraints keep projects focused
  • Aligns stakeholders: Everyone agrees on what success looks like before work begins
  • Reduces waste: Resources are allocated to what actually matters
  • Enables measurement: Defined metrics make it possible to track ROI

When to Use a Logic Audit

A Logic Audit is valuable at the start of any digital initiative:

  • Building a new website or platform
  • Implementing business systems (CRM, ERP, etc.)
  • Launching marketing campaigns
  • Planning digital transformation programs
  • Evaluating technology investments

It's especially critical for organizations in Rwanda and Africa, where resources are limited and every investment must deliver measurable impact.

The Prime Logic Approach

At Prime Logic, we conduct Logic Audits as the first step in every engagement. Our process includes:

  1. Discovery workshops: Facilitated sessions with key stakeholders
  2. User research: Understanding end-user needs and behaviors
  3. Context analysis: Mapping organizational constraints and capabilities
  4. Metrics framework: Defining measurable success criteria
  5. Recommendations report: Clear next steps and investment priorities

The result is a roadmap that aligns strategy, execution, and measurement—ensuring that every project starts with a solid foundation.
